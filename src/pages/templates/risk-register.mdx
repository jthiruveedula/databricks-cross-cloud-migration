# Risk register

[Risk assessment](/discovery/risk-assessment) scores workloads; the risk register is where medium- and high-risk items are tracked as living entries — owner, mitigation, status, and review date — rather than a one-time scoring exercise that gets forgotten after wave planning.

<Callout client:visible variant="warning" title="A risk register that's only reviewed once is just a report">
  The value of a risk register is in re-review: a mitigation that was "planned" at kickoff needs to actually get checked off before the wave it applies to executes. Set a recurring review cadence, not a single kickoff meeting.
</Callout>

## Register format

| Field | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Risk ID | Stable identifier for cross-referencing in wave plans and status reports |
| Description | What could go wrong, specifically — not a vague category |
| Risk tier | Low/Medium/High, from [risk assessment](/discovery/risk-assessment) scoring |
| Affected wave(s) | Which wave(s) this risk applies to |
| Owner | Single accountable person — cross-reference the [RACI](/templates/raci) |
| Mitigation | Concrete action(s) to reduce likelihood or impact |
| Status | Open / Mitigating / Mitigated / Accepted / Realized |
| Review date | Next scheduled check-in for this risk |

## Example entries

| Risk ID | Description | Tier | Wave | Owner | Mitigation | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| R-014 | Streaming pipeline checkpoint format incompatible with target storage | High | Wave 3 | Data eng lead | Validate checkpoint migration in pilot before wave 3 | Mitigating |
| R-021 | Orphaned Hive tables with no identified owner | High | Wave 2 | Platform lead | Time-boxed owner search; escalate to data governance if unresolved by wave start | Open |
| R-032 | BI tool connection strings hardcoded in dashboards, not centrally documented | Medium | Wave 1 | Analytics lead | Audit all dashboards for connection strings before cutover | Mitigated |
| R-045 | Compliance sign-off for PII-scoped catalog not yet scheduled | High | Wave 4 | Compliance lead | Schedule review 3 weeks ahead of wave 4 cutover | Open |

## Review cadence

- **Weekly** during active wave execution: review all Open and Mitigating items for waves in progress or starting soon.
- **At each wave boundary**: every risk tagged to the completing wave must be Mitigated, Accepted (with explicit sign-off), or explicitly carried forward with a reason.
- **At hypercare exit**: confirm no High-tier risk remains Open or Mitigating for the migration to be considered closed.

## Validation

- Confirm every High-tier risk has a named owner who has acknowledged it — an unowned high risk is itself a risk.
- Confirm mitigation actions are concrete and verifiable ("validate checkpoint migration in pilot"), not aspirational ("be careful with streaming pipelines").
- Cross-check realized risks (status: Realized) against the [rollback](/execution/rollback) log — a realized high risk that triggered a rollback should feed back into the risk model for remaining waves.

## Rollback

The risk register itself has no environment state to roll back — it's a tracking artifact. If a risk materializes into an actual incident, follow [rollback](/execution/rollback), then update the register's status to Realized and capture what was missed for the retrospective.

## Automation opportunity

- Auto-populate the register from risk-assessment scoring output (as shown above) instead of manual transcription.
- Send automated reminders for risks whose review date has passed without a status update.
